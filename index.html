What Is Low Carb Diet And What Are Its Benefits?

The typical low-carb diet does not have a fixed definition. It simply means having a low-carb or carb-restrcited diet. The diet usually emphasizes meats, fish, eggs, nuts, vegetables, seeds, fruits, and healthy fats. Also, you're supposed to minimize your intake of high-carbohydrates food such as grains, potatoes, sugary drinks, and high-sugar junk food. For a low carb diet, here are the recommended macro percentages:

  • Fat: 40 to 70%
  • Protein: 15 to 30%
  • Net carbs: 15 to 30%

Why should you consider eating fewer carbohydrates? In fact, there are many potential benefits, at all ages. The benefits of eating few carbohydrates include:

  • Lose weight
  • Increase longevity
  • Reverse your type 2 diabetes
  • Calm stomach
  • Less sugar cravings
  • Normalize blood pressure
  • Less Acne
  • Increased physical endurance
  • Less heartburn
  • Reduce symtoms of ADHD
  • Treating brain cancer
  • Mental Health
